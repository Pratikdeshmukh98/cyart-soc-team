Theoretical Knowledge:
The theoretical component establishes a strong foundation in Security Operations Center practices by covering essential cybersecurity concepts and frameworks. 
It includes understanding log analysis techniques such as correlation, anomaly detection, and log enrichment to identify suspicious patterns across multiple data 
sources. It also involves studying threat intelligence concepts like Indicators of Compromise, Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures, and their mapping using frameworks 
such as MITRE ATT&CK. Additionally, it covers structured incident handling processes based on standards like NIST SP 800-61, including alert triage, incident 
escalation, communication protocols, and evidence preservation. These concepts collectively provide the analytical and decision-making framework required to detect,
investigate, and respond to security incidents effectively.

Practical Knowledge:
The practical component focuses on applying theoretical concepts in a simulated SOC environment using industry-relevant tools. It involves performing hands-on 
activities such as ingesting and analyzing logs using the ELK Stack, simulating real-world attacks with Metasploit, validating threats through threat intelligence 
platforms, and managing incidents using tools like TheHive and CrowdSec. Practical tasks include alert triage, correlation of events, detection rule creation, 
incident escalation, and response actions such as containment and blocking malicious activity. It also includes evidence collection and documentation, ensuring proper 
chain of custody. This hands-on experience helps in developing technical proficiency, improving analytical skills, and gaining a realistic understanding of end-to-end
SOC operations.
